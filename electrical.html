Galactic ID Photobooth — Electrical Modifications Specification

Compiled from TSL ↔ Apple Industries correspondence (Dec 2025 – Apr 2026) & visual reference PDFs

1. Overview

The Galactic ID kit adds significant LED lighting and one AC-powered accessory (holographic fan) to the existing PHOTOMA mini platform. All decorative lighting runs on 12V DC (sourced from the booth's internal PSU), except for the side panel ID illumination strips which use 5V addressable RGB LEDs. The holographic fan runs on 120V AC via its own power supply routed through a dedicated extension cord.

Paul Ward (TSL) provided a detailed power calculation on April 1, 2026, estimating a total additional LED load of ~192W at 12V. On April 2, he recommended sizing up to a larger 12V PSU after a vendor substitution pushed COB strip wattage from 8W/m to 12W/m, leaving only ~8W headroom on the original calculation.

3. Power Budget Summary

Paul's calculation from April 1, 2026 (based on 8W/m COB strips):

⚠ PSU Sizing Alert — April 2, 2026

Paul's original 8W/m COB vendor fell through. Replacement strips are rated up to 12W/m (Amazon B0DHV85VCR, 6000K). At 12W/m, the pill light panels alone could draw ~264W instead of 176W, pushing total to ~280W+.

Paul demonstrated dimming these to ~9V with reduced current draw, but recommended sizing up the PSU for safety margin. With only ~8W headroom at the 8W/m calculation, a larger PSU is strongly advised.

Action required: Apple Industries needs to source a 12V PSU with sufficient headroom. At 12W/m worst case, target at least a 300–350W 12V PSU to provide 20% headroom.

4. PSU & Power Architecture

Existing PHOTOMA mini Power

The booth already has a 12V PSU powering existing electronics. The Galactic ID kit adds ~192–280W of 12V load on top of whatever the existing PSU supports.

Recommended Modification

Swap the existing 12V PSU for a larger unit — or add a dedicated second 12V PSU for the kit lighting. The PSU mounts inside the electronics cabinet. Given the worst-case 280W+ draw from the kit alone, a dedicated 300–350W 12V mean-well style PSU for the kit lighting is the safest approach.

Holographic Fan Power

The holographic fan runs on 120V AC and has its own power supply. A 10-foot 90° extension cord routes from the electronics cabinet, through the ceiling panel (8 screws to remove), up to the fan in the holo tower. The fan PSU can mount anywhere in the electronics panel.

5. Wiring Architecture

═══ 12V DC BUS (from PSU) ═══ │ ├── [Pill Light Panel L] ── 12V COB strip (11m, 6000K) ── single 12V lead │ ├── [Pill Light Panel R] ── 12V COB strip (11m, 6000K) ── single 12V lead │ ├── [Side Panel - Pills] ── 12V COB strip (24", white) ── tail 1 │ └── [Side Panel - RGB] ── WS2811/WS2812B (24") ── tail 2 (+ DATA signal) │ ├── [Instrument Panel] ── 12V COB strip (8") + 8× indicator bulbs │ └── All joined via Wago connectors → single 12V out │ └── [Chute Border] ── 9× 12V indicator bulbs ═══ 120V AC (separate) ═══ │ └── [Holo Fan] ── 10ft 90° extension cord ── through ceiling panel ── fan PSU in cabinet

6. Connector Strategy

TSL uses Wago lever connectors throughout the instrument panel wiring to join all LED leads to a single 12V connection. This allows tool-free disconnection without desoldering — critical for field serviceability. The same approach appears to be used on other panels.

The side panel has 2 separate tails exiting the back: one for 12V white COB, one for the addressable RGB strip. Each connects independently.

8. Open Questions & Decisions

  • PSU sizing: Need to confirm exact wattage of existing PHOTOMA mini PSU and determine whether to upgrade in-place or add a second dedicated supply for kit lighting.
  • COB strip actual draw: The 12W/m rated strips may draw less at 12V nominal. Paul demonstrated dimming to ~9V. Need to measure actual draw under typical operating conditions to right-size the PSU.
  • Pill light color temperature: 6000K confirmed for first 10 units but TSL noted it was initially "too blue." Warmer strip was auditioned. May revisit for production run.
  • Addressable LED controller: Apple Industries needs to determine what controller/driver will run the WS2811/WS2812B strips on the side panel — standalone controller, Arduino/ESP32, or integration with smileOS.
  • Instrument panel tile recess: Paul offered to sink tiles 1/16" deeper on production units to hide visible lip outline. Decision needed before production run.
