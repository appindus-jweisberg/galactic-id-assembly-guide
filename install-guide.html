On-Site Install Guide

Star Wars Galactic ID  |  Post-Shipping Field Installation

Overview

This guide covers installation steps performed at the customer location after the booth arrives from our facility. Certain components ship loose or detached for transit protection — this guide walks through securing them in sequence. Follow steps in order; each gate must be satisfied before proceeding.

Packed separately in the cabinet / crate: 4 sets of long and short box decorative shells & conduits, Holo Fan, 4 antennas, hardware bag (bolts, screws, self-tappers).

1

Position Booth & Level Legs

Move the booth to its final location and level it before attaching any external components.

1.1Position Booth at Final Location
Move the booth into its final operating position. Consider:
  • Power outlet access (120V)
  • Network access for modem
  • Ceiling clearance for the Holo Tower (adds ~18" above booth roof)
  • Door swing clearance (both large access doors on front)
  • Customer traffic flow and sightlines to the marquee
1.2Lower & Level Leg Feet
The booth ships with its leveling feet retracted and riding on casters. At its final position:
  1. Lower each of the 4 leveling feet by rotating them out of the base.
  2. Check level on both axes using a spirit level on the top ceiling surface.
  3. Adjust each foot until the booth is plumb and stable — it should not rock when pushed.
  4. Confirm the booth weight is transferred from casters to feet (casters should spin freely when the booth is level).
✓
GATE: Do not proceed to Step 2 until the booth is in its final position and fully leveled. Moving the booth after the Holo Tower and decorative shells are installed risks damage.
2

Install Long & Short Box Decorative Shells (4 Sets)

The 4 long/short box mounting plates ship pre-installed on the booth. The decorative shells and conduit hoses ship separately and are installed on-site.

💡
Each Galactic ID Photobooth has 4 sets of Long and Short Boxes, connected by plastic conduit. One set above and one below each large pill light panel (2 on each side of the booth).
2.1Verify Mounting Plates Are In Place
Before attaching shells, confirm the mounting plates are bolted to the booth (these were installed at the factory):
  • Long Box Mounting Plates — above and below each cabinet door. Secured with 3 × 1/4"-20 bolts per plate. Hose couplings face away from the cabinet door (up on top, down on bottom).
  • Short Box Mounting Plates (Top) — on the roof, one per side. Secured with 4 × 1/4"-20 bolts per plate. Hose couplings face outward.
  • Short Box Mounting Plates (Bottom) — on the underside, half boxes to clear leveling feet and casters. Secured with 2 × 1/4"-20 bolts per plate. Hose couplings face outward.

2.2Attach Conduit Hoses Between Long & Short Boxes
Each box set has two plastic conduit hoses connecting the long box to the short box.
  1. Secure each hose to the Short Box end first using one of the provided self-tapping screws and a powered driver.
  2. Bend the other end of the hose up (for upper sets) or down (for lower sets) to connect to the corresponding port in the Long Box Mounting Plate.
  3. Screw the hoses down into the Long Box couplings as well.
⚠
Bottom conduit hoses must be secured while the booth is on its side. If the booth is already upright and leveled (Step 1), you may need to lay it down again to access the bottom. Tip: if you know bottom conduits aren't attached yet, install them before doing final leveling.

2.3Slide & Secure Decorative Shells
Slide the corresponding decorative 3D-printed shell over each mounting plate/conduit assembly.
  1. Orient each shell correctly (note which is for the long box vs short box, and which side is up).
  2. Slide the shell over the mounting plate until the perimeter screw holes align.
  3. Secure each shell using the 3–6 perimeter screws:
    • Default: ⅜" 8-32 flat head screws
    • If any holes are too shallow: use ½" 8-32 flat head screws instead
  4. A hidden wood screw underneath each cosmetic shell holds the corresponding conduit in place — install this screw after the shell is seated.
💡
The cosmetic shells for the bottom short boxes can be installed with the unit upright. Use a powered screwdriver with an extension bit to reach the screws.

3

Ceiling Work — Remove Ceiling Panel, Install Holo Fan & Antennas

The Holo Tower ships with the Holo Fan detached, and antennas ship separately to avoid shipping damage. The ceiling panel must be temporarily removed to route cables.

3.1Remove Ceiling Panel
Open the booth and remove the ceiling panel by unscrewing its 8 fasteners. This exposes the ceiling cavity for cable routing.

Note: The ceiling panel was last reinstalled at the factory (Phase 5 Step 5.9). You may find USB and holo fan power cables already routed through the ceiling cavity from factory work.
3.2Install the Holo Fan
The Holo Fan ships separately from the booth. The Holo Tower's outer shell, base flange, fan mount and tubes were pre-installed at the factory — your job on-site is to install the fan itself inside the already-mounted tower.
  1. Locate the fan and verify the right-angle DC extension cord is pre-routed up through the tower's mounting stem (factory-installed, strain-relieved at the Spacer Block). If not, run it up through now.
  2. Align the central hub of the Holo Fan with the large circular hole in the Front Shell of the tower.
  3. Pass the extension cord through the Front Shell and plug it into the fan via the JST connector. The fan is powered by the dedicated 12V line from the booth — the fan will begin spinning automatically once power is applied in Step 3.4.
  4. Lift the fan (and its attached Front Shell, if not yet secured) and align the fan's mounting slot with the cleat on the Fan Mount. Slide down together so the fan locks onto the cleat.
  5. Attach the Front Shell to the Shell Base with the 2 × ½" 8-32 screws at the bottom of the shell.
  6. Re-attach the Back Shell using the 8 × 8-32 screws.

3.3Mount Holo Tower Base to Booth Roof
(Skip this substep if the factory already mounted the Holo Tower to the booth roof — inspect the roof for the tower's base flange bolted through the 6 rivet nuts.)
  1. Route the DC extension cord down out the base of the Holo Tower and into the center hole in the top of the Photobooth.
  2. Run the cord through the framing under the ceiling panel and down to the right side cabinet, where it will plug into the 12V power supply.
  3. Slide the Holo Tower Assembly onto the roof of the booth and align the 6 holes around the perimeter of the base flange with the 6 rivet nuts in the top of the booth.
  4. Secure with 4–6 × 1/4"-20 bolts. The 6th hole directly under the Holo Fan Shield is difficult to reach when installed and is not necessary for a secure attachment — omit if necessary.

3.4Install Antennas (x4) & Route to Modem / Payment Device
The 4 antennas ship separately inside the cabinet or in separate packaging. Install and connect them to the modem and payment device.
  1. Locate the 4 antenna holes drilled in the booth roof per the Roof Template (2 original + 2 offset .551" holes).
  2. Thread each antenna's base through its corresponding hole from the outside, and secure with its locking nut from the inside.
  3. Route the 4 antenna cables through the ceiling cavity:
    • 2 antenna cables → Modem (mounted between the speakers on the modem plate). Connect to the modem's SMA connectors.
    • 2 antenna cables → Payment Device (ePort / eVend or equivalent). Connect to the payment device's antenna ports.
  4. Verify LAN and power are reconnected to the modem and payment device.
⚠
Do not over-tighten antenna locking nuts. Hand-tight plus a quarter turn with a wrench is sufficient — over-tightening can strip the thread or crack the antenna base.
3.5Reinstall Ceiling Panel
Once all cables are routed and verified, reinstall the ceiling panel with its 8 screws. Verify no cables are pinched by the panel or its framing.
✓
GATE: Holo Fan spins up automatically when powered; all 4 antennas connected; modem and payment device online; ceiling panel reinstalled with no pinched cables.
4

Verify All Lighting Components

Power on the booth and confirm every LED / illuminated element functions as expected. Compare against the reference visuals below.

4.1Power-On Sequence
  1. Plug the booth into its 120V outlet.
  2. Turn on the primary power switch.
  3. Wait ~30 seconds for PSU 1, PSU 2, controllers (LC 1 and LC 2), modem, and computer to come up.
  4. Observe the boot sequence — LEDs should illuminate in their default/startup state.

Walk around the booth and confirm each lighting element is functioning. Reference the hero image below for expected appearance.

4.315-Minute Burn-In
Leave the booth powered on for at least 15 minutes with all LEDs active. Monitor for:
  • Flicker or dimming — suggests undersized PSU or loose connection
  • Overheating — touch-test PSU housings and COB strips (warm is OK, hot is not)
  • Holo fan noise — should be a steady hum, no grinding or wobble
  • Camera function — take a test photo; confirm no lens occlusion from the camera housing cover
✓
GATE: All lighting elements from the checklist are functioning; 15-minute burn-in passed with no flicker/overheat; camera test photo taken successfully. Installation complete.
