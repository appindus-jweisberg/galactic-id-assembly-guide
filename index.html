Phase 0 — Preparation & Staging

Gather tools, verify kit contents, and prepare the Card Creator booth before making any modifications.

🛈
This guide converts an existing Card Creator photo booth into a Star Wars Galactic ID photo booth using the Galactic ID conversion kit. Contact: Jesse Weisberg (jesseweisberg@appleindustries.com) with any questions.
✓
STEP 0.1
Verify Required Tools Are Staged
Confirm the following tools and consumables are available at the workstation. All items below are required — the templates call out specific bit/hole sizes that must be matched exactly.

Power Tools:
• Drill / drill press with the following bit sizes:
 — .551" drill bit (antenna holes — per "Antenna Hole .551"" on roof template)
 — 1" drill bit or hole saw (holo fan power — per "Holo Fan Power Hole 1"" on roof template)
 — 3/4" drill bit or hole saw (side panel power injection — per "Power Injection 3/4"" on side panel template)
 — 5/8" drill bit (door — per "5/8" Hole" on door template)
 — 1/2"+ drill bit (pill light power injection — per "1/2"+ Power Injection Pill Light" on pill light middle template)
 — 6-32 clearance drill bit (#27 or 9/64") — camera housing, instrument panel, and door template holes
 — 8-32 clearance drill bit (#18 or 11/64") — side panel and door template holes
 — 1/4"-20 clearance drill bit (17/64" or "F" letter bit) — pill light panel mounting holes
 — 1/4"-20 rivet-nut pilot drill bit (3/8" or per rivet-nut manufacturer spec) — roof, long box, short box
• Angle grinder with cut-off wheel (oval/rectangle cuts on instrument panel and door)
• Reciprocating saw with metal blade (keyhole slots, rectangle slots, arrow lock slot)

Hand Tools & Hardware Install Tools:
• 1/4"-20 rivet-nut installation tool
• Screwdriver set (Phillips + flathead)
• Tape measure • Marker / scribe / center punch
• Clamps (to hold acrylic templates against booth surfaces)
• Metal file / deburring tool

Safety: Safety glasses • Work gloves • Ear protection • Dust mask

Phase 2 — Cabinet Modifications (Cuts & Holes)

Use the laser-cut acrylic templates to precisely locate all cuts, holes, and slots. Templates are labeled with specific hole sizes and registration points. Clamp templates securely before marking/drilling.

⚠
Measure twice, cut once. These are permanent modifications. Always align template registration points ("Register to Screen", "Register to Speaker", "Register to Key Slot", "Register to Existing Screw Holes", "Register to Lock", "Register to Joint", "Register to Side") before marking any holes. Center-punch all drill locations.
✓
STEP 2.1
Roof Template — Antenna Holes, Holo Fan Hole, Rivet-Nut Pilots & Slots
Position the red roof template (P/N 55786) on the top of the booth.

Antenna Holes: Locate all holes labeled "Antenna Hole .551"" (4 positions — 2 original, 2 offset). Drill using a .551" drill bit. Center-punch first.

Holo Fan Power Hole: Locate "Holo Fan Power Hole 1"". Drill using a 1" hole saw or drill bit. Routes the holo fan power extension cord from the short box into the electronics cabinet.

Rivet-Nut Pilots & Slots: Drill all "1/4-20 Rivet Nut" positions using a 3/8" pilot bit (multiple positions). Cut the rectangular/oval slots — drill pilot holes at corners first, then connect with the reciprocating saw.

Phase 3 — Surface Preparation

With all cabinet cuts complete, prepare the bare booth surfaces before installing hardware.

STEP 3.1
Apply White Vinyl Backing to Door Interior & Side Panel Area
Critical for LED effect: Apply white vinyl (or white material) to: (1) the inside of the booth door behind where the pill light panels will mount, and (2) the booth side wall behind where the side panel will mount. This white surface bounces light back through the acrylic diffusion for the pill lights and side panel LEDs. Without this, the LED effect will be significantly diminished.
