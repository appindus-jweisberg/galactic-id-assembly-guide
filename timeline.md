# Galactic ID Photobooth — Fabrication Timeline
## TSL (The Specialists LTD) × Apple Industries

**Compiled from email correspondence: Paul Ward (TSL Project Manager) ↔ Jesse Weisberg (Apple Industries)**
**Date range: December 1, 2025 — April 9, 2026**

---

## Phase 1: Initial Contact & Concept Development
### Dec 1–12, 2025

**Dec 1** — Jesse reaches out to TSL via contact form. Describes Apple Industries as a photo booth manufacturer for Disney parks, movie theaters, museums, etc. Looking for a fabrication partner for custom enclosures and scenic elements for the PHOTOMA mini, to create a "Star Wars Galactic ID" machine. Initial target: 100+ units by end of March 2026, timed for the Mandalorian film release.

**Dec 2** — Nate Kolbeck (TSL Director, Client Services) schedules intro call for Dec 3.

**Dec 4** — Post-call: Nate requests NDA and CAD files. Paul Ward (TSL Project Manager) is cc'd from this point forward.

**Dec 5** — Jesse sends countersigned NDA and PHOTOMA mini STEP file (`PHM_Vendor.step`), confirming Lucasfilm meeting for Dec 11.

**Dec 11** — Paul delivers two concept render packages:
- **"Dropship" Concept**: Imperial-styled starship with deep vacuum-formed 3D panels on front, back, and top. More expensive due to deep dimensional parts. Paul notes vacuum-formed panels for big exterior parts at 100-unit scale could run $300–$600/unit including tooling.
- **"In-World Kiosk" Concept**: Shallower, boxier panels — envisioned as an in-world ID kiosk ("space self-service DMV"). Cheaper due to shallower vacuum-formed parts.
- Paul also sends a quick revision: Dropship Rev1 with a top wing cowling to change the silhouette further.
- Fabrication approach: vacuum-formed plastic for big 3D exterior panels, CNC/laser-cut for shallower interior panels, real machined aluminum for levers and instrument panels. LEDs assumed to be flexible placement (strips diffused through white acrylic).

**Dec 11 (evening)** — Jesse reports Lucasfilm meeting went well. They loved both concepts, especially the In-World Kiosk which inspired a "passport droid" idea — an ID/passport droid you could enter and operate.

**Dec 19** — Paul checks in, mentions further vacuum-forming pricing work done, standing by for design direction.

**Dec 22** — Jesse updates: Lucasfilm on holiday break, leaning away from in-world utility droid and toward something more commercial/minimal (closer to tradeshow prototype). Interest in holographic projection display on top. Also presented an R2-themed projector alternative. Design direction still in the air.

## Phase 2: Design Direction Confirmed & Proposal
### Jan 15 – Feb 12, 2026

**Jan 15** — Paul checks in post-holidays, notes lower costs expected for the flatter panel designs vs. the earlier "certainly spaceship" concept.

**Jan 26** — Jesse sends Lucasfilm-approved "design direction": the simpler/commercial look. Shares design brief PDF (`StarWars_GalacticID_Cabinet_Presentation_20260125.pdf`). Requests TSL fabrication cost estimate with material/finish options. Apple will source the holographic fan separately. Timeline: 20–30 sets by mid-March, 70–80 more in April.

**Jan 30** — Paul acknowledges, targeting per-unit kit cost at or below $5,000, asks for timeline requirements.

**Feb 4** — Jesse presses: ASAP timeline, hoping simpler design brings cost below $5k/unit.

**Feb 10** — Paul confirms interest, working through build strategies and numbers.

**Feb 12** — **Major milestone: Paul delivers full proposal.** Includes working mockup model, overview drawing, and render document with components and materiality callouts. Key details:

### Kit Scope (per unit):
| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| 2× "Pill" Lighting Panels | Aluminum cowling with milk acrylic diffusion |
| 1× Speaker Covering Panel | CNC aluminum border + black acrylic cover + vacuum-formed speaker rings |
| 1× Instrument Panel | CNC aluminum border + black acrylic cover + lit acrylic button tiles + LED panel-mount lights + non-functional electronic greeblies |
| 1× Display Covering Panel | CNC aluminum border + black acrylic cover + machined aluminum light greeblie + camera shroud |
| 2× Side ID Display Panels | CNC aluminum border + black acrylic cover + backlit red acrylic pill lights + hardware for mounting ID samples |
| 1× Print Chute Border | Vacuum-formed silver-gray plastic + panel-mount LEDs + machined aluminum indicator light |
| 4× Box and Hose Assemblies | Gray vacuum-formed boxes with embossed panel detail + connecting hoses |
| 1× Hologram Fan Tower Housing | 2-piece vacuum-formed silver-gray plastic shell around provided holo fan |

### Cost Estimates:
| Phase | Estimate |
|-------|----------|
| Design | $9,000–$13,000 |
| Prototype / First Unit | $25,000–$35,000 (3–4 weeks from design approval) |
| Vacuum Mold Tooling | $40,000–$55,000 (4 molds × $10k–$15k each) |
| Scale Production (100 units, with LEDs) | $5,000–$5,500/unit |
| Scale Production (100 units, without LEDs) | $4,000–$4,500/unit |

**Note:** Quantities of 20–40 may see 25–30% cost increase on some components. Prototype vacuum-formed parts to be 3D printed first for fit testing before investing in molds.

## Phase 4: Technical Design & Initial Prototyping
### Mar 11–18, 2026

**Mar 11** — Paul sends progress update with attached render/photo document:
- **Technical design finishing up**, ready to start adding elements to booth on-site.
- **3D printed prototypes** of large dimensional shells in production.
- **Holo Tower Update:** Revised design from aesthetic review call — eliminates negative space between towers and side pipe for cleaner vacuum-formed shell trimming. Updated renders provided.
- **Side Panel Lighting:** Prototype built with backlighting for ID cards. Milk-white acrylic diffusion with continuous white LED strip. Video demo included (`Galactic ID_Side Panel Lighting Demo_Rev1_03.10.2026.mp4`). Options: different diffusion color (e.g., cool blue for "hologram" effect), separate LED leads for ID illumination vs. red pills.
- **Panel Mounting Cutouts:** Near-final cutout specifications for Instrument Panel and Side Panel mounting into photobooth body. Many drilled holes and rivet nuts required (templates to be provided).
